# Ionospheric Conductivity During Storm vs. Quiet Periods

## Scope of Study
This research compares ionospheric behavior during intense geomagnetic storm periods with relatively quiet times. The goal is to understand how solar activity and geomagnetic disturbances interact with ionospheric conductivity.

## Significance
Solar storms can disrupt:
- Satellite communication and navigation systems
- Power grids and railway networks
- Submarine cables
- Astronaut health in space missions

By analyzing ionospheric dynamics, this study contributes to space weather mitigation strategies and enhances predictive modeling for technological resilience.

## Methodology
- **Study Design**: Comparative analysis of storm days (Oct 29–31) vs. quiet days (Oct 4, 8).
- **Data Collection**: Parameters include:
  - Conductivity types
  - Electron and ion temperatures
  - Geomagnetic indices (Kp, Dst)
- **Simulation Tools**:
  - Kyoto WDC datasets
  - CCMC IRI system
- **Analysis**:
  - Statistical comparison of conductivity peaks
  - Spatial and temporal dynamics
  - Altitude variations across ionospheric layers
